## Direct quotes from Shahar Chen (verbatim)

### On his background
"I grew up in Israel, with a significant portion of my childhood dedicated to a dual focus: indulging my passion for soccer (and apparently not being too successful in that) and deconstructing computers simply to understand how they work."

### On discovering AI / the founding insight for Aquant
"Honestly, it was AI who found me! My business partner and I identified an unresolved issue within the service sector — one rooted in the widening skills gap — and recognized that the solution lay in harnessing the existing data of companies."

### On current projects at Aquant
"Delivering a holistic generative AI solution for the service industry while implementing stringent security measures to prevent internet uploads is truly an exhilarating undertaking."

### On his co-founder relationship (Assaf Melochna)
"Our synergy extends beyond mutual complementarity and a seamless understanding conveyed through mere eye contact."

### On engaging a conservative industry (the central differentiation framing)
"When starting a career in any industry, you have to recognize and begin understanding the people you'll be working alongside. And when it comes to a conservative industry like ours, understanding the human element, the concerns and even fears — is super critical. To many people, AI is a scary word. A service technician might hear the word AI and their immediate thought is, 'They're introducing a new system to take over my role.' AI is not here to replace anyone, AI is here to help us be the best we can be at what we do."

### On the five career essentials (each shorter quote)
1. "Building a strong technical foundation is crucial."
2. "The AI field evolves rapidly, with new techniques and technologies emerging regularly."
3. "The ability to identify problems that AI can solve and design effective solutions is vital."
4. "Effective communication skills are necessary to convey complex ideas to non-technical stakeholders."
5. "Understanding the potential biases, privacy concerns, and broader societal implications of AI is essential."
